CASE BRIEF: ALL NUTRITION (A)

Strategic Briefing

Segmentation & Omni-Channel CX Transformation in Chile

The Strategic Pivot

Context & Challenge

All Nutrition (35 stores + online) faces commoditization in the fragmented Chilean market. The goal is to pivot from a "product supply" retailer to a "best-in-class omni-channel customer experience" provider, using expertise to defend margins.

The Dilemma

Founders must scale operations based on data, not intuition. However, the current data foundation (3 qualitative focus groups) is insufficient for a fleet-wide restructuring.

Recommendation: Pause & Validate
Do not execute the rollout yet. The risk of misaligning staffing and inventory based on unproven segments is too high. Proceed with a 60-day quantitative validation phase.

The Flawed Research Foundation

The current strategy is built on exploratory qualitative focus groups. Using this to define the entire market creates a high risk of operational failure due to significant biases.

1. Selection Bias

Recruiting at gyms and stores heavily skews results toward "Heavy Users," completely missing the massive "Novice/Wellness" market opportunity.

2. Sample Size (N=24)

Three groups are statistically insignificant. The resulting 6 segments are anecdotes, not validated market clusters.

3. Moderator Bias

Using an internal company psychologist risks confirmation bias rather than neutral probing.

Customer Psychology & Motivations

Despite the sampling bias, the sessions revealed critical psychological drivers that should inform the quantitative phase and CX design.

The Core Insight: The Credibility Gap
Customers inherently distrust sales staff ("commission chasers") but implicitly trust Trainers and Athletes. The new CX model fails if staff act like clerks instead of coaches.

Critique: From Theoretical to Operational

The proposed 6 segments (e.g., "Newbie Cautious," "Health Compelled") are descriptively rich but operationally unmanageable for store staff. They violate MECE principles (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive).

We must collapse these into practical service models based on Needs and Willingness-to-Pay for advice.

Critical Assumption Check: We assume the "Novice" segment is large enough and willing to pay a premium for advice to justify the higher labor costs of the Omni-CX model. If they are price-sensitive, the model fails.

Recommendations & Implementation Roadmap

Shift from immediate rollout to a phased "Validate-Pilot-Scale" approach.

Metrics & Decision Gates

  • Research Gate: Silhouette Score > 0.5 (Are the segments statistically real?)
  • Commercial Gate: Pilot store Basket Size +15% (Does advice cover the extra labor cost?)
  • Experience Gate: NPS parity between "Novices" and "Experts."

Immediate Next Steps (Week 1)

  • Draft quantitative survey instrument focusing on Needs vs. Knowledge.
  • Identify 2 pilot locations (one high-traffic, one neighborhood).
  • Freeze all current store remodeling plans.
